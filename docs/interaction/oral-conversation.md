<details>

  <summary>Detailed explanation</summary>

This descriptor indicates that the individual is capable of handling very short social exchanges but is rarely able to understand enough to keep a conversation going on their own accord. However, they can be made to understand if the interlocutor takes the trouble to assist them.

They can use simple, everyday, polite forms of greeting and address. This means they can engage in basic social interactions, such as saying hello and using appropriate forms of address in a respectful manner.

They can converse in simple language with peers, colleagues, or members of a host family, asking questions and understanding answers relating to most routine matters. Additionally, they can make and respond to invitations, suggestions, and apologies. They are also able to express their feelings using basic stock expressions and state their preferences by expressing what they like and dislike.

</details>
